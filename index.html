About Me

As of August 2025, I am working in the Theory of Atoms research group of Prof. Ludmil Katzarkov at Institute of Mathematics and Informatics at the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences, Sofia, Bulgaria. I received my PhD in December 2020 from Loughborough University, UK, my advisor was Prof. Hamid Abban.

My research interests include birational geometry of singular Fano varieties, especially nonrationality problems and Sarkisov links. Fano varieties are central in geometry, generalising the sphere to higher dimensions. Fano varieties also have close connections to physics, as the anticanonical sections of Fano varieties provide the main construction of Calabi–Yau manifolds used in string theory. In a recent work, inspired by ideas from mirror symmetry, the nonrationality of very general cubic fourfolds was proved using the theory of atoms. While in Sofia, I plan to study the theory of atoms, its applications to nonrationality questions and its connections to noncommutative spectra. I am also interested in computer algebra and I am one of the developers of the OSCAR computer algebra system.
